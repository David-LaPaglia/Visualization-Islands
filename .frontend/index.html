Visualization Islands

Research Focus: This study investigates how the hosting site impacts the accessibility and usability of COVID-19 visualizations.

Challenge: Visualizations are often isolated by the accessibility and performance limitations of the hosting websites.

WARNING: I am still working on this and do not really know what Information to put down!

Original Research Questions

1. How effective is a visualization that is specifically for accessibility if it is hosted on an inaccessible website?

2. How valuable are visualization tools for the web if they are hosted on websites that take minutes too cumbersome for many people to access?

3. How do hosting site accessibility and performance affect the usability of public health visualizations for diverse user groups?

Goal:

Identify barriers and propose solutions to enhance accessibility and usability for diverse users.

Motivation / Background

Widespread Accessibility Issues: 96.3% of top websites fail to meet accessibility standards (WebAIM, 2023).

Digital Divide: 23% of U.S. adults lack broadband, with rural and low-income groups facing greater access challenges (Pew, 2021).

Only 57% of households earning under $30,000 have broadband, compared to 92% of higher-income households (Burrel, 2018).

Problem

If similar barriers hold for websites that host important public data visualizations, then the design of our charts may act as visualization islands - data portraits that are inaccessible due to their technical context, regardless of how compelling, accessible, or well-crafted the visualization itself is designed.

Methods

Dataset: COVIC Archive – Over 3,000 COVID-19 visualizations from various sources (news, social media, peer-reviewed studies).

Accessibility Assessment: WebAIM’s WAVE API – Analyzed 3,136 sites for accessibility issues, identifying types and frequency of WCAG failures.

Performance Assessment: WebPageTest – Collected load time data across different connection types (3G, DSL, FIOS).

Methodology: Combined automated accessibility and performance analysis to evaluate technical barriers in accessing COVID-19 visualizations.
